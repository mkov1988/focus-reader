# Marketing Plan: House Calculator → Focus Reader

## 1. The Core Strategy: Side-Project Marketing via "UX Leadership"

The goal is to use the **House Calculator** as a high-utility lead magnet to build an audience that eventually converts to **Focus Reader**. Because the audiences (real estate math vs. focused reading) do not naturally overlap, the bridge is **Michael Kalmykov, the Creator**. 

Your core narrative is: *"I am a People-first UX Leader who builds calm, high-quality, distraction-free software."* 

The calculator acts as the undeniable proof of that ethos. It provides immense upfront value, builds trust, and establishes your authority. Once that trust is established, you introduce them to your flagship product: Focus Reader.

---

## 2. The Conversion Funnel Mechanics

To make this work, the user journey must be frictionless at the start, and highly intentional at the end.

### Step 1: The Frictionless Hook
*   **Action:** The calculator must be instantly usable. Zero pop-ups, zero cookie banners, zero "Sign in with Google" walls blocking the primary UI. 
*   **Why:** You are competing with Zillow and Bankrate. Your competitive advantage is speed and lack of friction. If you gate it, they will bounce immediately.

### Step 2: The Value-Exchange Capture (Bottom of Calculator)
*   **Action:** Once the user completes a calculation and sees their amortization/investment graph, present a highly contextual capture form at the bottom of the results.
*   **Primary Copy Example:** *"Want to save this scenario to compare later? Enter your email and I'll send you a secure link to this exact calculation."*
*   **Alternative Copy:** *"I build one free, distraction-free tool like this every month. Join my newsletter to get the next one."*

### Step 3: The "Trojan Horse" Welcome Email
*   **Action:** When they enter their email, the first automated email delivers exactly what was promised (the saved link). But the P.S. section introduces the bridge.
*   **Email Structure:**
    *   *Here is your saved calculator scenario: [Link]*
    *   *I built this tool because I got frustrated by how bloated and ad-infested modern web calculators are.*
    *   *My philosophy as a UX designer is that software should be calm, fast, and respect your attention.*
    *   *P.S. If you appreciate that philosophy, you might like my main project, **Focus Reader**—an app designed to help you read without distraction. [Link]*

## 3. Distribution Playbook (The Content Engine)

### Platform A: LinkedIn (Primary Audience Building)
LinkedIn is for building your "UX Leader" authority. You are targeting other professionals, designers, and founders who appreciate good software (and are prime candidates for a focus/productivity app).

**Content Frameworks:**
1.  **The "Teardown" Post:** 
    *   *Visual:* A split image. Left: A messy, ad-filled mortgage calculator. Right: Your clean UI.
    *   *Body:* Explain exactly *why* the bad UI causes user anxiety (cognitive load, dark patterns). Explain your specific UX decisions to fix it. 
2.  **The "Build in Public" Post:** 
    *   *Visual:* A chart of your traffic, or a screenshot of your Figma file.
    *   *Body:* "Why I spent 40 hours building a free mortgage calculator." Talk about side-project marketing, the value of giving away free tools, and how it fits into your larger goal of building Focus Reader.

### Platform B: Reddit (Spike Traffic)
Reddit requires extreme caution to avoid self-promo bans. You must provide 90% value and 10% promo.
*   **Target Subreddits:** `r/UXDesign`, `r/webdev`, `r/SideProject`, `r/FirstTimeHomeBuyer`.
*   **The "Feedback" Strategy:** Never post a naked link. Post a text thread explaining the technical or design challenges you faced. 
    *   *Example Title for r/webdev:* "I built an open-source House Calculator because the SEO spam ones drove me crazy. Here's how I handled the compounding math in JS."
    *   *Call to Action:* "I hosted it ad-free on my portfolio [Link]. I'm a UX designer by trade, so I'd love if you guys could roast the UI and tell me what I missed."

## 4. The "Portfolio Hub" Architecture (`mkalmykov.com`)

The calculator cannot exist in a vacuum. It must be hosted directly on your portfolio (e.g., `mkalmykov.com/calculator`).

**Required Hub Elements:**
1.  **Global Header:** Your site navigation must persist on the calculator page. `Michael Kalmykov | Focus Reader | Tools | Articles`. This immediately shows users there is more to explore.
2.  **The "Creator Plaque":** Below the calculator, include a small bio section. 
    *   *Copy:* "Built by Michael Kalmykov. A People-first UX Leader based in Atlanta. Want to see what else I'm building? Check out Focus Reader."
3.  **Cross-Linking:** Ensure Focus Reader has a dedicated landing page on your hub, making it easy for calculator users to click over and see the app's value prop.

---

## 5. Success Metrics (What to Measure)

Don't just launch and guess. Track these specific KPIs to know if the bridge is working:
1.  **Top of Funnel:** Unique visitors to the Calculator page.
2.  **Capture Rate:** What percentage of users click "Save my scenario" and input an email? (Goal: 2-5%).
3.  **Bridge Click-Through:** What percentage of welcome-email recipients click the link to Focus Reader?
4.  **Hub Conversion:** How much traffic flows from `mkalmykov.com/calculator` to `mkalmykov.com/focus-reader` via the navigation/footer?
